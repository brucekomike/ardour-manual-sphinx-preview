The mini-timeline allows, as the [Summary](@@summary) does, navigating a
session. Its main advantage, though, is that it stays visible even when
in Mixer mode.

The range of time covered by the mini-timeline is set by [right]{.kbd
.mouse} clicking the timeline, and choosing a time span from 30 seconds
up to 20 minutes.

The mini-timeline shows all markers (*start*, *end* and any user defined
ones). Clicking a marker jumps to that point on the timeline, allowing
for quick access to key timings in the session. The next and previous
markers out of the mini-timeline range are indicated by arrows, as the
*end* marker in the screenshot above.

[Cue markers](@@mixing-linear-nonlinear-workflows) are also displayed,
with their play time duration shown by a line.

While hovering with the mouse over the mini-timeline:

- [left]{.kbd .mouse} clicking moves the playhead to the time under the
  mouse cursor
- using the [scroll wheel]{.kbd .mouse} scrolls the playhead back and
  forth inside the session
- using []{.kbd .mod1}[scroll wheel]{.kbd .mouse} scrolls more finely
  inside the session
- using []{.kbd .mod1}[]{.kbd .mod2}[scroll wheel]{.kbd .mouse} scrolls
  even more finely inside the session
